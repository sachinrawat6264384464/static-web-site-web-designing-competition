Blood clotting

Blood clotting, or coagulation, is the body's natural mechanism to stop bleeding from an injured blood vessel. When a blood vessel is damaged, platelets and clotting factors in the blood activate, forming a platelet plug and then a mesh of fibrin threads that traps red blood cells and solidifies into a clot to seal the wound. Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disease where high blood sugar levels result from the body not producing enough insulin or not using it effectively, leading to serious damage to organs like the heart, eyes, kidneys, and nerves. The most common form is Type 2 diabetes, linked to lifestyle factors such as excess weight and inactivity, while Type 1 is an autoimmune condition where the pancreas produces little to no insulin. Management involves healthy eating, regular physical activity, and, for many, medication or insulin to control blood glucose levels and prevent severe healthÂ complications.
